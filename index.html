SV-GaSRelight: Single-View Gaussian Splatting for 3D Human Relighting

SV-GaSRelight.

Abstract

In virtual environments, where dynamic and interactive 3D content is essential, realistic lighting plays a crucial role in enhancing immersion. However, traditional relighting methods often rely on multiview captures or computationally expensive neural rendering techniques, making them impractical for real-time applications. In this work, we propose SV-GaSRelight, a novel single-view 3D scene relighting approach specifically designed for human relighting. Our main contribution is to exploit human bodies in the scene as 3D probes that can help to decompose lighting conditions from surface albedo and orientations. This is possible thanks to very powerful models that are able to reconstruct 3D human bodies from a single view. Our pipeline reconstructs a 3D human from a single input image and creates synthetic multiple views with camera poses. Then, we use Relightable 3D Gaussian, which incorporates BRDF decomposition and ray tracing, to simulate realistic lighting effects. Relightable 3D Gaussian uses Neural Incident Light Field (NeILF) to introduce lighting information in the conventional 3D Gaussian Splats. We evaluate our method by comparing results with Relightable 3D Gaussian Splatting which requires multiview input. We also evaluate performance by conducting a user study to evaluate the scene's realism. Our experimental results show that we can achieve comparable results with relightable 3D Gaussian Splatting.
